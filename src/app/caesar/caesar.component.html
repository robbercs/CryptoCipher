Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher is a substitution cipher method in which each letter in the original text is shifted by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet. This shift is known as the "key" and determines the transformation of the letters. While it is one of the simplest encryption techniques, the Caesar Cipher illustrates the fundamental principle of cryptography: the transformation of data to protect its confidentiality. However, due to its simplicity, the Caesar Cipher is vulnerable to brute-force attacks and is not considered secure for protecting sensitive data in modern applications.
